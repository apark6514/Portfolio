About

Hello! My name is Andrew Park and I am a sophomore in Mechanical Engineering the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign planning to graduate in December 2026. I'm extremely passionate about combustion and propulsion systems as well as robotics and have had extensive experience in both fields.

During the Summer of 2024 I had the privilege of working at Argonne National Laboratory in the Transportation and Power Systems Division where I wrote scripts to analyze combustion data from sustainable fuel combustion tests. This summer, I will be interning at Caterpillar in the Alt Fuels Group working on fuel injector systems and engine performance. Click here to learn more about my experience!

When it comes to projects, I've been lucky enough to have a variety of experiences that have helped me cultivate many skills in various disciplines. I'm currently leading a 6-axis Robotic Arm project with over 30 members as well as a Liquid Rocket Engine Analysis team that performs both thermal and structural analysis on the combustion chamber and nozzle of a liquid rocket engine. I've done many other projects as well, and you can view the full list here.

While I am very technically focused, I'm also a real human and greatly value my free time. I love music, exercise, and Jesus and have played piano for 14 years. I also play the violin and am slowly learning the guitar. I love the outdoors and hiking, which is part of the reason I'm so excited to study abroad in Switzerland in Fall 2025! Feel free to learn a little more about me here.

Last but not least, I'm currently searching for a co-op in the aerospace or robotics industries for the Spring 2026 semester. I'd love to connect on LinkedIn, and I also frequently check my email linked in my resume, so please reach out with any opportunities or questions!
